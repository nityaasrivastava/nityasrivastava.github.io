Aerobatic Aircraft Design

Weight Estimation

1. Although MTOW (Maximum Takeoff Weight) is the absolute upper limit of the weight the aircraft can sustain, it increases aircraft weight and reduces maneuverability, thereby affecting performance.

2. Therefore, the design is based on the design takeoff gross weight \( W_0 \), not MTOW.

Design Equation

$$ W_0 = W_{crew} + W_{payload} + \left(\frac{W_f}{W_0}\right)W_0 + \left(\frac{W_e}{W_0}\right)W_0 $$

$$ W_0 - \left(\frac{W_f}{W_0}\right)W_0 - \left(\frac{W_e}{W_0}\right)W_0 = W_{crew} + W_{payload} $$

$$ W_0 = \frac{W_{crew} + W_{payload}}{1 - \left(\frac{W_f}{W_0}\right) - \left(\frac{W_e}{W_0}\right)} $$

Definitions

  • W₀: Design takeoff gross weight
  • Wcrew: Crew weight
  • Wpayload: Payload weight
  • Wf: Fuel weight
  • We: Empty weight

📘 Theory: Iterative Estimation of Takeoff Weight

The objective of this calculation is to determine the design takeoff gross weight (W₀) of the aerobatic aircraft. This weight represents the total aircraft weight at the beginning of the mission and is a key parameter in aircraft design.

The governing relation for weight estimation is given by:

$$ W_0 = \frac{W_{crew} + W_{payload}}{1 - \left(\frac{W_f}{W_0}\right) - \left(\frac{W_e}{W_0}\right)} $$

In this equation, the empty weight fraction (Wₑ/W₀) and fuel weight fraction (W_f/W₀) are not known exactly beforehand and depend on the aircraft configuration. These values are initially estimated using historical data for similar aircraft.

Since W₀ appears on both sides of the equation, a direct solution is not possible. Therefore, an iterative method is used. An initial guess of W₀ is assumed, and the equation is evaluated repeatedly until the difference between successive values becomes sufficiently small.

The aim of this process is to obtain a converged value of W₀, such that:

$$ |W_0^{new} - W_0^{old}| < \text{tolerance} $$

For the present analysis, a typical aerobatic aircraft (similar to Sukhoi Su-26) is considered, and appropriate weight fractions are assumed. The convergence behavior is monitored using absolute and percentage error.
