Dragonfly Micro Air Vehicle

Bio-inspired flapping-wing aerial robot

Objective

Design and analyze a dragonfly-inspired micro air vehicle capable of stable hovering and efficient flapping-wing flight.

Engineering Approach

  • Four-wing dragonfly configuration (fore & hind wings)
  • Gear-driven flapping mechanism using coreless DC motors
  • Crank-rocker based wing actuation
  • Lightweight 3D-printed thorax frame

Aerodynamics & Physics

  • Leading-edge vortex based lift generation
  • Clap-and-fling wing interaction
  • Unsteady aerodynamic forces

Analysis & Simulation

  • ANSYS-based structural modeling of thorax
  • Modal and vibration analysis of wing mechanism
  • MATLAB-based motion and frequency analysis

Outcome

This project demonstrates application of aerospace dynamics, structures, and bio-inspired aerodynamics to micro-air-vehicle design.
