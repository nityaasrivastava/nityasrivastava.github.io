Lunar Lava Tubes

Subsurface habitats and exploration pathways on the Moon

Objective

To study lunar lava tubes as potential natural shelters for future human and robotic exploration, providing protection from radiation, micrometeoroids, and extreme temperature variations.

Scientific Background

Lunar lava tubes are underground tunnels formed by ancient volcanic activity. These structures can span several kilometers and offer stable thermal environments, making them strong candidates for permanent lunar bases.

Engineering Relevance

  • Radiation shielding for astronauts and equipment
  • Structural stability of underground caverns
  • Thermal insulation from lunar day-night cycles
  • Safe deployment of habitats and rovers

Analysis & Approach

  • Geometric modeling of lava tube cross-sections
  • Stress and stability considerations for cavern roofs
  • Thermal behavior of subsurface environments
  • Potential rover navigation inside lava tubes

Applications

Lava tubes could be used as natural underground bases for long-term human missions, fuel storage, scientific labs, and radiation-protected habitats on the Moon and Mars.
